Growth came in waves. Expansion accelerated in Epoch 5 and peaked in Epoch 7, then slowed sharply in the final stretch of v1. That surge was driven in large part by more deliberate community onboarding, especially the rollout of Sablier-based distribution and partnerships such as SheFi and Celo.

Methodology: New users are defined here as addresses appearing in Octant v1 budget data for the first time in a given epoch. This measures entry into the budgeted user base, not necessarily first observable allocation activity.

How the 279 active E10 allocators moved through the final funnel
Migration funnel from E10: 279 active E10 allocators → 266 remained eligible in E11 → 122 allocated in E11 → 112 migrated to Octant v2.
Drop-off happened before migration, not during it. Of 279 Epoch 10 allocators, 95.3% remained eligible for Epoch 11 — but only 43.7% chose to allocate again. Of those who did allocate, 91.8% went on to migrate to v2, confirming that the committed core transferred almost entirely.
